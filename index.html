About

When we created this device, we had one thing in mind, Safety. With the specific sensors we used, we were able to create a device that needs no contact to work. The sensors we used are: temperature sensor, ultrasonic sensor, motion sensor and lastly a motor driver. Look below to see what each sensor does.

The MLX90614 is an infrared thermometer for non-contact temperature measurements. ... As a standard, the 10-bit PWM is configured to continuously transmit the measured temperature in range of -20 to 120°C, with an output resolution of 0.14°C.

An ultrasonic sensor is an electronic device that measures the distance of a target object by emitting ultrasonic sound waves, and converts the reflected sound into an electrical signal. Ultrasonic waves travel faster than the speed of audible sound (i.e. the sound that humans can hear).
